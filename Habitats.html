Why Habitats Are Important

Habitats are important because they provide animals and plants with everything they need to survive, such as food, water, and shelter. Each habitat has special conditions that help different living animals grow and stay healthy. When habitats are protected, animals can live safely and continue their life cycles. Habitats also help keep nature balanced by supporting food chains and controlling the climate. If habitats are destroyed, many animals would lose their homes and become endangered. That's why, protecting habitats is important for the survival of wildlife and for the future of our planet.


Habitats

Forest

Forests are full of trees and plants. They provide shelter and food for many animals.

Ocean

Oceans cover most of the Earth. They are home to many sea creatures.

Desert

Deserts are very dry and hot. Animals here save water to survive.

Grassland

Grasslands have wide open spaces with grass and few trees.

Polar habitat

Polar regions are very cold and covered with ice. Animals there have thick fur or fat to stay warm.

Mountain

FMountains are high and cold land areas. Animals here are strong climbers.

Wetlands

Wetlands are areas with a lot of water and soft land. They are home to frogs, birds, and fish.






Forest

  • Forests are covered with many tall trees and thick plants.
  • They usually have good rainfall and rich soil.
  • The ground is often cool and wet.
  • Animals can hide and build homes there.
  • Many insects and birds live in forests.
  • Examples: monkeys, tigers, parrots, frogs, butterflies, and ect.


Ocean

  • Oceans are large areas of salt water.
  • They cover about 70% of Earth's surface.
  • Some parts are shallow, while others are very deep.
  • Sunlight only reaches the top layer.
  • Water temperature changes from warm to very cold.
  • Many aquatic animals breathe under the water using gills.
  • But some animals come up for air.
  • Examples: fish, dolphins, whales, turtles, sharks, and ect.


Desert

  • Deserts are very hot and dry.
  • There is very little rain.
  • The land is sandy or rocky.
  • There are few plants, mostly cactus and bushes.
  • Water is hard to find.
  • Many animals prefer to come out at night.
  • Examples: camels, lizards, snakes, scorpions, and ect.


Grassland

  • Grasslands are wide open areas with tall grass.
  • There are few trees.
  • Fires can sometimes happen naturally.
  • Many animals live in groups.
  • Predators hunt in open spaces.
  • Examples: lions, zebras, giraffes, elephants, grasshoppers, and ect.


Polar habitat

  • Polar habitats are near the North and South Poles.
  • They are covered with snow and ice.
  • Temperatures are extremely cold.
  • There is little plant life.
  • Some months have long daylight, others long darkness.
  • Animals have thick fur or fat.
  • They can survive in freezing weather.
  • Examples: polar bears, penguins, seals, Arctic foxes, and ect.


Mountain

  • Mountains are very high land areas with steep slopes.
  • SThe air is thin and cold, especially at the top.
  • Weather changes quickly and it often snows.
  • Lower parts may have forests, while higher parts have rocks and ice.
  • Plants are small and strong because of wind and cold.
  • Animals have to be good climbers.
  • Examples: mountain goats, snow leopards, eagles, yaks, and ect.


Wetlands

  • Wetlands are places where land is covered with water.
  • The land is often muddy or flooded.
  • Plants grow in shallow water.
  • Many birds and frogs live here.
  • They help clean water naturally.
  • Examples: frogs, ducks, fish, insects, turtles, and ect.
